Most Popular

Musculoskeletal Physiotherapy

The most common form of physiotherapy, treating conditions affecting muscles, joints, tendons, ligaments, and nerves throughout the body.

What it involves

A thorough assessment of the affected area, surrounding structures, and contributing factors. Treatment typically combines hands-on techniques with a tailored home exercise programme and education about your condition.

Conditions treated

  • Lower back & neck pain
  • Sciatica & nerve pain
  • Hip & knee pain
  • Shoulder & elbow conditions
  • Foot & ankle problems
  • Arthritis & joint pain
  • Muscle strains & tears
  • Tendinopathies (tennis elbow, Achilles, etc.)

3

4

Follow-up sessions every 1–2 weeks

Sports Injury Rehabilitation

Specialist rehabilitation for sports-related injuries, designed to get you back to your sport safely, quickly, and stronger than before.

What it involves

A sport-specific approach combining injury assessment, manual therapy, progressive loading, and return-to-sport protocols. I work around your training schedule and performance goals — not just pain relief.

Conditions treated

  • Ankle & knee ligament sprains
  • Hamstring & quad strains
  • Stress fractures & bone stress
  • Runner's knee (PFPS)
  • Shin splints (MTSS)
  • Rotator cuff injuries
  • Achilles tendinopathy
  • Groin & hip flexor injuries

4

Full return-to-play clearance

Post-Op Care

Post-Surgical Rehabilitation

Structured, evidence-based rehabilitation following orthopaedic surgery to restore full strength, mobility, and function safely.

What it involves

Phase-based rehabilitation following your surgeon's protocol, progressed according to your healing, strength, and functional milestones. I liaise with your surgical team to ensure a safe, coordinated recovery.

Surgeries supported

  • ACL & PCL reconstruction
  • Knee & hip replacement
  • Rotator cuff repair
  • Shoulder labrum repair
  • Spinal decompression & fusion
  • Ankle ligament reconstruction
  • Meniscal repair
  • Fracture fixation

Recovery Phases

1

Early post-op: swelling & pain control

2

Restore mobility & basic strength

3

Progressive strength & endurance

4

Hands-On Treatment

Manual Therapy

Skilled, hands-on techniques to reduce pain, restore joint movement, and improve tissue function — often providing immediate relief.

What it involves

A range of evidence-based hands-on techniques applied to joints, muscles, and soft tissues. Manual therapy is typically used alongside exercise and education for best long-term results.

Techniques used

  • Joint mobilisation & manipulation
  • Soft tissue massage
  • Myofascial release
  • Trigger point therapy
  • Neural mobilisation
  • Dry needling / acupuncture
  • Mulligan & Maitland techniques
  • Taping & strapping

Best suited for

✓

Prevention & Performance

Movement Assessment

A comprehensive biomechanical screening to identify how you move, find weaknesses or imbalances, and prevent injury before it happens.

What it involves

A full-body movement screen analysing posture, joint mobility, muscle strength, movement patterns, and biomechanics. You receive a detailed written report with prioritised recommendations.

Who it's for

  • Recreational & competitive athletes
  • People with recurring injuries
  • Those returning to exercise
  • Desk workers with persistent pain
  • Anyone wanting to move better
  • Pre-season screening
  • Post-rehab performance check
